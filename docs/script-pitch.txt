# PRISM Pitch Video Script (2 Min)

## Slide 1 — Intro

Crypto rebuilt trading.

It rebuilt exchanges.
It rebuilt payments.
It rebuilt liquidity.

But one massive market is still broken on-chain.

Credit.

We’re building PRISM — Programmable Risk & Income Structured Markets.

A protocol that turns credit into structured, tradable risk layers on Solana.

---

## Slide 2 — The Problem

Today, on-chain credit has two big problems.

First — users don’t really understand what risk they’re taking.

Second — credit positions are illiquid.

Most protocols either:

* overcollateralize everything
* or isolate loans into separate markets

And that creates fragmented liquidity, poor pricing, and inefficient capital.

It feels like traditional finance problems copied on-chain.

---

## Slide 3 — Real World Analogy

But in traditional finance, banks don’t sell loans one by one.

They pool loans together.

Then split them into different risk layers.

Low risk.
Medium risk.
High risk.

That’s how structured credit works.

We realized:

the problem isn’t tokenizing loans.

The problem is structuring risk.

---

## Slide 4 — Our Solution

That’s what PRISM does.

Users deposit into one pooled credit vault.

Then choose their exposure:

Prime — lowest risk
Core — balanced risk
Alpha — highest risk

Each layer becomes a tradable SPL token.

So instead of choosing borrowers,
users simply choose risk.

---

## Slide 5 — Hero Moment

Yield flows top-down.

[pause for visual]

Losses cascade up.

Alpha wipes. Core takes a hit. Prime — protected.

---

## Slide 6 — Real Money

Three users. Same vault. Different tranches.

After the default —

Prime walked away with twenty dollars in profit.

Core lost a thousand.

Alpha lost everything.

That’s not the protocol failing.

That’s the protocol working exactly as it promised.

---

## Slide 7 — Live Markets

And unlike traditional credit markets, users don’t need to wait until a loan matures to exit.

For example, imagine you invested 10 dollars into pCORE in a 90-day pool.

If, on day 30, the pool is performing well and borrowers are repaying normally, the value of your pCORE tokens may increase because yield has already started accruing.

You can simply sell those tokens on the AMM and exit early with profit.

But if defaults happen and losses reach the Core layer, the market price of pCORE can fall.

So when you exit, you may leave with less than you started with.

That’s the key difference:

PRISM doesn’t lock risk behind fixed maturity.

It lets markets continuously price it in real time.
